\section{Data}

This section will describe the tax survey panel, financial statement variables, industry classification, input-output tables, and the concordance between firm industries and IO industries.

The main variable definitions are fixed in the shared variable dictionary. The key objects are the downstream wedge \(\psi^X_{d,t}\), the IO transaction \(Z_{d,u}\), the upstream sales-destination weight \(\omega_{u \to d}\), and the upstream exposure measure
\[
  Exposure_{u,t} = \sum_d \omega_{u \to d}(\psi^X_{d,t} - 1).
\]

The weight \(\omega_{u \to d}\) is defined from the upstream supplier's perspective: the share of upstream industry \(u\)'s sales going to downstream industry \(d\). This distinction is central because the project studies pressure transmitted to upstream suppliers, not the downstream industry's input composition.

The empirical analysis also constructs control variables that distinguish downstream-wedge exposure from competing mechanisms. These include upstreamness, upstream SOE share, upstream market concentration, upstream SOE concentration, own upstream markup, upstream profitability, export exposure, and firm lifecycle controls such as productivity, size, and age. When data permit, the robustness analysis will add processing-trade or production-line-position proxies to separate the main mechanism from GVC upgrading.
